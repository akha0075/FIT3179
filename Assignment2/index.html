Energy Generation In The United States

by Aayan Khan

Energy Generation By State

The US is home to 50 states, each of which have their own governing bodies and resources. Thus, it is no surprise that these states have vastly different energy generation methods. Some states like Tennesee generate over 43 percent of their energy from nuclear whereas other states like Indiana generate none of their energy from nuclear.



Total Energy Consumption and Per Capita Energy Consumption

Total U.S energy consumption has gradually increased over time along with the population, however, the energy consumed per person has remained flat over the past 40 years. This is likely due to the increases in efficiency of appliances as well as transport.

We can clearly see a sudden decline in the total energy consumption as well as the per capita consumption in 2020 due to COVID-19.

Change Over Time (All)

While the total energy generation has been growing as a whole, we can clearly see that some industries have outpaced others over the past years.

Coal was dominant twenty years ago but has since lost the crown to natural gas which is now the most common type of energy generation method used in the US today.

Other industries like nuclear and renewables have also gained significant traction over the past fourty years while petroleum has slowly been phased out.



Change Over Time (Renewables)

Renewables have been steadily increasing production these past few years. Wind has especially increased energy generation since 2010 while hydroelectric has maintained its output at a steady pace.

We can also see that solar is slowly being adopted more and more while industries like geothermal and biomass have slightly shrunk.

We will likely see continued adoption of renewables as climate change becomes an increasingly important issue.

Consumers Of Energy

Transportation and Industrial sectors account for the majority of energy consumption in the US as they consume thirty-seven percent and thiry-five percent of the generated energy resepectively. Similarly, Residential consumers utilize about 16 percent of the generated energy while Commercial consumers use up the remaining twelve percent.

Overall Energy Production

While states may differ vastly from each other, the overall composition of energy generation in the US often tells the same story. The majority of energy in the United States is generated using Coal and Natural Gas while Nuclear produces about twenty percent. Finally, about fifteen percent is generated through renewable means.

Energy Prices By State and Dominant Sector

The price of energy can vary severely from state to state and is impacted by a multitude of factors. These can include things such as the median income in the state or the cost of living. Furthermore, the population may impact the demand for energy in the state. Ultimately, the source of energy also plays a vital role in the pricing of energy as some industries are cheaper to operate than others.
